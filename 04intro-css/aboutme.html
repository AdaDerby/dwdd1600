Here's a little bit about me...

History

I'm a 22 year old currently living in Utah but I was raised in Virginia! I come from a big family. As the eldest of five kids I was the experiment. My parents tested everything out on me first, and for that I am super grateful because one of those experiments was an art class! I was able to find my passion for art at a very young age and it has stuck with me for my whole life. Whether it was doodling on my homework or testing out every different art medium I could get my hands on, I've been making art and working out my creativity for my whole life.

Academics

I have a long history of making art throughout my education, even if it wasn't always for a formal art class. When I wasn't nose deep in a book I was working on the sets for my middle school's plays in the advanced art club or putting way too much effort into an extra credit art assignment for English class. I viewed my love of art as a fun hobby, but didn't consider it as a career prospect until my senior year. I had a gap in my schedule and decided to pick up a fun elective for my final year of high school.

That graphic design class opened my eyes to just how needed art is in the modern day. I was in love with everything about graphic design. I immediately saw a future where I could create art for a living. Package design, branding, website building‐ all of it was fascinating. I knew that this had to be my major in college. I've learned a lot since that first class, and I've loved every second of it. I found my passion and never looked back.

Goals

My goals moving forward are pretty simple. I plan to graduate with a degree in either Art and Design or Graphic Design from Utah Valley University in the coming years and use my hard earned skills in my future career. Graphic design is a very versatile field, and I am attracted to many different possible specialties and applications. I hope to curate a portfolio that shows off the wide variety of skills I've learned.

As of now, my greatest strength is my love of the creative process. I just adore those first steps of a project where you dive into a topic headfirst, brainstorming and researching, trying things and exploring possibilities. If I were not in this field, I would be in some sort of humanities degree. I've always been fascinated with the human experience and graphic design marries that fascination with art so beautifully.
